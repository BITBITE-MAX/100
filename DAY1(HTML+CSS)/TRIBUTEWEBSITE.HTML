Wikipedia


Wikipedia is a free online encyclopedia, created and edited by volunteers around the world and hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation.

About Wikipedia

☛ Wikipedia is a free, web-based, collaborative, multilingual encyclopedia project supported by the non-profit Wikimedia Foundation.
☛ It is written and maintained by volunteers around the world, known as Wikipedians.
☛ Wikipedia was launched on January 15, 2001, by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger.
☛ It was initially intended as a complementary project to Nupedia, an expert-reviewed free encyclopedia, but quickly became the main focus.
☛ Wikipedia contains millions of articles in hundreds of languages, covering a vast range of topics from science and technology to arts and culture.
☛ Articles are organized into categories and often contain links to related articles, enabling easy navigation.
☛ Anyone with internet access can edit almost any page on Wikipedia, except for protected pages.
☛ The community of editors follows a set of guidelines and policies to maintain the quality and neutrality of content.
☛ Wikipedia’s open editing model has been both praised and criticized. While it allows for rapid updates and a diverse range of contributions, it can also lead to issues with accuracy and vandalism.
☛ The reliability of Wikipedia varies by article, and users are encouraged to check citations and references for verification.
☛ Wikipedia is one of the most visited websites globally, serving as a valuable resource for information.
☛ It has had a significant impact on the accessibility of knowledge, enabling free and widespread access to information.
☛ Wikipedia is part of a larger ecosystem of projects under the Wikimedia Foundation, including Wikimedia Commons, Wikidata, and Wiktionary.
☛ Wikipedia continues to evolve with ongoing efforts to improve content quality, expand language coverage, and incorporate new technologies such as machine learning for better content moderation and curation.
